Frequently Asked Questions

  • Do I need any prior programming experience?

    For the beginner workshop you don't need any prior Rust experience. However, you should be familiar with another programming language like Python, JavaScript, Java, C, C++, etc. For the advanced workshop you should have a good understanding of the Rust basics and be familiar with the Rust ecosystem.

  • How does the live mentorship work?

    Live mentorship involves real-time sessions with experienced Rust developers who guide you through the workshop material and answer your questions.

  • What if I can't attend the live sessions?

    All live sessions are recorded and made available to students. You can also ask questions in our Discord server.

  • Do I get a certificate?

    Yes, you get a certificate of completion after finishing a workshop. The certificate mentions the workshop name, the level, the duration of the workshop, your name, and the date of completion. You can use the certificate to present to your employer or add to your resume.

  • What if I can't afford the workshop?

    The workshops are open source and available for free. However, we recommend you to enroll in the workshop to get the most out of it. We offer a limited number of scholarships for students who can't afford the workshop. Please get in touch.

  • What if I'm not satisfied with the workshop?

    If you're not satisfied with the workshop, we'll refund your money. No questions asked.
